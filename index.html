Story

How I think and what I care about

I like complexity, but I like clarity more. My work is usually somewhere between technical systems and the humans who have to use them.

I work in research, tech and a bit of sales because I want to understand how humans and systems behave and why people say yes. I have studied how people think about digital and physical surveillance, built experiments, analysed data and turned it into insight that normal humans can understand without a degree.

Now I sit inside the world of Amazon account management, managing sellers and seeing how decisions look when money, risk and time pressure are all in the room. I want to bridge the gap between technical thinking, data and commercial reality instead of leaving them on different sides of the building.

I like tools that make people feel more confident to do their job, and I like building the things I wish I had when I was learning something.

Work

Things I have built and helped grow

Some live inside companies, some in research, some on the internet. All of them taught me something about how people and systems behave.

Lumi ‚Äì AI seller call simulator

Internal training concept and prototype for Amazon style seller conversations.

Lumi is a training tool that lets new account managers practice seller calls in a safe environment. It simulates a seller, responds in near real time and can evaluate how the call went. I designed the conversation flow, built the front end in HTML, CSS and JavaScript, connected it to n8n for workflow automation and wired in AWS services to handle speech input, dialogue logic, knowledge base access and scoring.

Conversation design HTML / CSS / JS n8n automation AWS (Polly, Transcribe, LLMs)

Vellaro ‚Äì AI fencing analysis

Early stage product concept for performance feedback using pose estimation.

Vellaro is a machine learning based tool that analyses fencing footage and turns movement into insight. I defined the product concept for an AI based fencing feedback tool that uses pose estimation, conducted interviews with fencers and coaches to understand UX expectations and mapped the feature progression from raw pose data to movement metrics and tactical feedback that athletes can act on.

Pose estimation Product discovery Sports analytics User interviews

Fechtklingen.de ‚Äì Online fencing store

Niche ecommerce for fencing equipment, specialised on blades.

Fechtklingen is an online fencing equipment store with a focus on blades and related gear. I helped build the early category expansion strategy, including product diversification and supplier relations, and contributed to brand vision and positioning as the store expanded beyond blades. It taught me a lot about conversion, logistics and what people actually care about when they buy equipment.

Ecommerce Category strategy Supplier relations Brand building
